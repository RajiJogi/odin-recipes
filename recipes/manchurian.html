Manchurian

Description

Manchurian is arguably the crown jewel of Indo-Chinese cuisine — soft, spiced vegetable dumplings (or balls) deep-fried to a crispy finish and then dunked in a tangy, umami-packed sauce with garlic, ginger, and chillies. Despite the name, it has no real connection to the Manchuria region of China; it was invented in Mumbai in the 1970s and has since become a beloved fixture of Indian street food. Served either dry or in gravy, it's bold, saucy, and utterly addictive.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups finely grated cabbage (squeezed dry)
  • 1 carrot, finely grated
  • 3 spring onions, finely chopped
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoons ginger, grated
  • 4 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (for balls)
  • 0.5 teaspoons black pepper
  • 0.5 teaspoons salt
  • 3 tablespoons oil for deep frying
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce (for sauce)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons chilli sauce
  • 1 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 tablespoons cornstarch mixed with 3 tbsp water (slurry)
  • 0.5 cup water or vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 green chillies, slit
  • 1 tablespoons sesame oil

Steps

  1. Make the veggie balls: In a large bowl, combine 2 cups finely grated cabbage (squeezed dry), 1 carrot, finely grated, half of 3 spring onions, finely chopped, half of 5 garlic cloves, minced, 1 tablespoons ginger, grated, 4 tablespoons cornstarch, 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons soy sauce (for balls), 0.5 teaspoons black pepper, and 0.5 teaspoons salt. Mix well until the mixture holds its shape when pressed. Form into small balls (about 1 inch in diameter).
  2. Deep fry the balls: Heat 3 tablespoons oil for deep frying in a deep pan or wok to 170°C (340°F). Fry the balls in batches for 3–4 minutes until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
  3. Mix the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together 3 tablespoons soy sauce (for sauce), 2 tablespoons tomato ketchup, 2 tablespoons chilli sauce, 1 tablespoons vinegar, and 0.5 cup water or vegetable broth. Set aside.
  4. Build the gravy base: Heat 1 tablespoons sesame oil in a wok. Add the remaining 5 garlic cloves, minced and 3 green chillies, slit and stir-fry for 2m 30s . Add 1 onion, finely chopped and sauté for 2 minutes.
  5. Thicken the sauce: Pour the sauce mixture into the wok and bring to a simmer. Stir in 1 tablespoons cornstarch mixed with 3 tbsp water (slurry) and cook for 1–2 minutes until the sauce thickens to a glossy consistency.
  6. Toss and serve: Add the fried balls to the sauce and toss gently to coat. Garnish with the remaining 3 spring onions, finely chopped and serve immediately.
