Chilli Paneer

Description

Chilli Paneer is a popular Indo-Chinese dish where crispy, golden cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) are tossed in a bold, fiery sauce made with soy sauce, chilli, and aromatic vegetables. It's a staple at street stalls and restaurants across India, loved for its smoky wok-kissed flavour and satisfying texture contrast between the crunchy exterior and soft interior of the paneer. It can be served dry as an appetizer or with a bit of sauce as a main course alongside fried rice or noodles.

Ingredients

  • 400 grams paneer, cubed
  • 4 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 green bell pepper, cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, cubed
  • 1 onion, cubed
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 green chillies, slit
  • 2 tablespoons tomato ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons chilli sauce
  • 1 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 3 tablespoons oil for frying
  • 0.5 teaspoons salt
  • 0.5 teaspoons black pepper
  • 2 spring onions, chopped (garnish)

Steps

  1. Coat the paneer: In a bowl, mix 4 tablespoons cornstarch and 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour with a pinch of 0.5 teaspoons salt and 0.5 teaspoons black pepper. Toss 400 grams paneer, cubed in this mixture until evenly coated.
  2. Fry the paneer: Heat 3 tablespoons oil for frying in a wok or pan over medium-high heat. Shallow-fry the coated paneer cubes until golden and crispy on all sides. Remove and set aside on paper towels.
  3. Sauté aromatics: In the same wok, add 1 tablespoons sesame oil and sauté 5 garlic cloves, minced and 3 green chillies, slit on high heat for 3 minutes . Add 1 onion, cubed, 1 green bell pepper, cubed, and 1 red bell pepper, cubed and stir-fry for 2–3 minutes, keeping the vegetables slightly crunchy.
  4. Make the sauce: Add 3 tablespoons soy sauce, 2 tablespoons tomato ketchup, 2 tablespoons chilli sauce, and 1 tablespoons vinegar to the wok. Stir well and let it cook for 1 minute . If you prefer a saucier version, add a splash of water mixed with a teaspoon of cornstarch.
  5. Toss and finish: Add the fried paneer to the sauce and toss everything together on high heat for 2 minutes until well coated and glossy. Garnish with 2 spring onions, chopped (garnish) and serve hot.
