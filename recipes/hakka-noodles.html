Hakka Noodles (हक्का नूडल्स)

Description

Hakka Noodles are an Indo-Chinese classic — thin, chewy noodles stir-fried on a screaming-hot wok with vegetables, soy sauce, and a hint of vinegar. The dish traces its roots to the Hakka Chinese community that settled in India, who adapted their traditional cooking to local tastes. The result is something uniquely its own: smoky, savory, slightly tangy, and endlessly satisfying. It pairs beautifully with Chilli Paneer or Manchurian as part of a full Indo-Chinese spread.

Ingredients

  • 300 grams Hakka noodles (or thin egg noodles)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 tablespoons chilli sauce
  • 1 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 green chillies, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cabbage, thinly shredded
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 3 spring onions, whites and greens separated
  • 0.5 teaspoons black pepper
  • 0.5 teaspoons salt
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 green chillies, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cabbage, thinly shredded
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 3 spring onions, whites and greens separated
  • 0.5 teaspoons black pepper
  • 0.5 teaspoons salt

Steps

  1. Cook the noodles: Cook 300 grams Hakka noodles (or thin egg noodles) in a large pot of boiling salted water according to package directions until just al dente. Drain, toss with a little 2 tablespoons oil to prevent sticking, and set aside.
  2. Prep the sauce: In a small bowl, combine 3 tablespoons soy sauce, 1 tablespoons vinegar, 1 tablespoons chilli sauce, and 1 tablespoons sesame oil. Mix well and set aside.
  3. Stir-fry aromatics: Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a wok over the highest heat possible. Add 4 garlic cloves, minced, 2 green chillies, finely chopped, and the whites of 3 spring onions, whites and greens separated. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add vegetables: Add 1 carrot, julienned, 1 cabbage, thinly shredded, and 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced to the wok. Stir-fry on high heat for 2–3 minutes , keeping the vegetables crisp.
  5. Toss with noodles: Add the cooked noodles to the wok. Pour the sauce over and toss everything together vigorously on high heat for 2 minutes . Season with 0.5 teaspoons black pepper and 0.5 teaspoons salt.
  6. Garnish and serve: Turn off the heat and finish with the green tops of 3 spring onions, whites and greens separated. Serve hot immediately.
